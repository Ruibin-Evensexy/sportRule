Soccer Rules

This entry contains FIFA soccer rules, covering team size, field, ball, match time, kick-off, scoring, fouls, winning, and other requirements.

Number of Players

Each team has 11 players, including one goalkeeper, with up to three substitutes.

Field Dimensions

The field is 100–110m long, 64–75m wide; goals are 7.32m by 2.44m.

The Ball

The ball measures 68–69cm in circumference and weighs 410–450g.

Match Duration

A match has two 45-minute halves with a 15-minute half-time; extra time is two 15-minute halves.

Kick-Off

A coin toss decides which team starts, and the match begins with a kick-off.

Scoring

A goal counts as one point, and play restarts from the center line afterward.

Fouls and Penalties

Handball is a direct free kick, offside is indirect, and two yellow cards equal a red.

Winning

The team with more goals wins; ties are resolved with penalties if needed.

Other Rules

Players must respect referees and opponents, and cheating or violence is forbidden.

Number of Players

A match requires at least seven players to start. Teams may register up to 11 players, including one goalkeeper, and use up to three substitutes during a match.

Field Dimensions

The field must be rectangular, 100–110m long and 64–75m wide. Goals are centered at each end, 7.32m wide and 2.44m high, with corner flags and marked penalty areas.

The Ball

The ball must be made of suitable material, keep its shape, and weigh 410–450g with a circumference of 68–69cm.

Match Duration

Matches are 90 minutes plus half-time. If tied, extra time is two 15-minute halves, followed by penalties if needed.

Kick-Off

Before kickoff, a coin toss decides the starting team. Kick-off is taken from midfield into the opponent’s half, and play restarts from the center after each goal.

Scoring

A goal is worth one point. After a goal, the opposing team restarts the match from the center spot.

Fouls and Penalties

Handball results in a direct free kick. Offside is indirect unless in the defending penalty area. Two yellows equal a red, and the penalized player leaves the field.

Winning

The match winner is the team with the higher score. Extra time and penalty kicks decide ties when necessary.

Other Rules

Players must respect referees and opponents. Goalkeepers may use hands inside their penalty area but not outside. Substitutions require referee approval and must follow the substitution zone rules.
